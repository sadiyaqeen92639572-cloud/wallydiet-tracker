Why Third-Party Testing Matters for Supplements

The FDA does not approve dietary supplements before they hit shelves. That means the label on your protein powder or creatine is essentially self-reported by the manufacturer. Independent testing by organizations like NSF International, USP, and Informed-Sport is the only way to verify what is actually in the product.

Studies have found that up to 30% of supplements contain ingredients not listed on the label, including banned substances, heavy metals, or underdosed active ingredients. Third-party tested products have passed rigorous checks for label accuracy, contaminant levels, and manufacturing quality.

Our Trust Score combines certification status, label transparency, filler detection, and consumer ratings into a single 0–100 number so you can compare at a glance.

Trust Score formula: 40pts (third-party cert) + 20pts (rating) + 15pts (verified servings) + 15pts (open label) + 10pts (no fillers) = 100 max

Supplements by Category

We track five major supplement categories. Each has different quality signals to watch for.

🥛 Whey Protein

Perfect for: muscle recovery, meal replacement, and meeting daily protein goals. Look for whey isolate over concentrate for higher protein per scoop. Key red flags: proprietary blends, amino spiking, and maltodextrin filler.

Popular searches: grass fed whey protein, NSF certified whey, cleanest whey protein powder, whey isolate vs concentrate

⚡ Pre-Workout

Perfect for: energy, focus, and pump during training. Clinically dosed citrulline (6-8g), beta-alanine (3.2g), and caffeine (150-300mg) are the benchmarks. Avoid proprietary blends that hide under-dosed ingredients behind a total weight.

Popular searches: clean pre workout no artificial sweeteners, sucralose free pre workout, best third party tested pre workout

💪 Creatine

Perfect for: strength, power output, and cognitive function. Creatine monohydrate is the most studied form — fancy variants (HCl, buffered) offer no proven advantage. Best value: unflavored powder in bulk. Check for Creapure sourcing and third-party purity testing.

Popular searches: creatine monohydrate third party tested, cheapest creatine per serving, NSF certified creatine, creapure creatine

💧 Electrolytes

Perfect for: hydration during exercise, keto diets, and hot-weather performance. Key electrolytes: sodium (1000mg+), potassium, and magnesium. Watch for sugar-loaded formulas marketed as "hydration" — true electrolyte mixes have minimal or zero sugar.

Popular searches: electrolyte powder no sugar, LMNT alternative, clean electrolyte mix, best electrolytes for keto

💊 Multivitamins

Perfect for: filling nutritional gaps, especially on restricted diets. USP or NSF certification is critical here — multivitamin potency degrades over time, and independent testing confirms the labeled dose is still accurate at expiration. Avoid mega-doses above 100% DV for most nutrients.

Popular searches: USP verified multivitamin, best third party tested multivitamin, clean multivitamin no fillers

Supplement FAQ

Answers to the most common questions about supplement quality, testing, and value.

What does third-party tested mean for supplements?

Third-party tested means an independent lab — not the supplement company — verified that the product contains what the label claims, is free from harmful contaminants like heavy metals and pesticides, and meets quality standards. The most respected certifiers are NSF International, USP, and Informed-Sport. Products without any third-party testing rely entirely on self-reported label claims.

What is a proprietary blend and why should I avoid it?

A proprietary blend lists ingredients without disclosing individual amounts — you only see the total weight of the blend. This means you cannot verify if citrulline is dosed at the clinically effective 6g or at a useless 500mg. Transparent labels show exact doses for every ingredient. Over 60% of pre-workouts use proprietary blends to hide under-dosing.

How is the Trust Score calculated?

Trust Score (0-100) weighs five factors: third-party certification (40 points), average consumer rating normalized to 20 points, verified serving count (15 points), open label vs proprietary blend (15 points), and absence of common fillers like maltodextrin or magnesium stearate (10 points). A score above 70 indicates a highly trustworthy product.

What is the cheapest way to buy creatine per serving?

Bulk unflavored creatine monohydrate powders offer the lowest price per serving, often under $0.15. Brands like BulkSupplements and Nutricost sell 1kg tubs with 200 servings for under $20. Always verify third-party testing — at this price point, purity matters more than flavor. Avoid creatine HCl or buffered creatine variants that charge 3-5x more with no proven advantage.

Is NSF certification better than USP for supplements?

Both are rigorous. NSF Certified for Sport is preferred by competitive athletes because it also screens for 270+ banned substances. USP Verified focuses on potency, purity, and dissolution. For general consumers, either certification means the product passed independent testing — both are far better than no certification at all.

Are sucralose-free pre-workouts less effective?

No. Sucralose is an artificial sweetener with zero impact on workout performance. Sucralose-free pre-workouts typically use stevia or monk fruit instead. What matters for effectiveness is the dose of active ingredients like citrulline (6-8g), beta-alanine (3.2g), and caffeine (150-300mg) — not which sweetener is used.
